Kake Udon

Description

Served in a delicate umami broth, this classic Udon Noodle Soup (or Kake Udon) is a bowl of true comfort.

Simple yet full of soul, it is a thing of beauty. Enjoy the noodle soup as is or top it with tempura, fish cake, tofu, or vegetable

Ingredients

  • 2 servings udon noodles (6.3 oz/180 g dry udon noodles; 1.1 lb/500 g frozen or parboiled udon noodles
  • 2⅓ cups dashi (Japanese soup stock; click to learn more) (or 2⅓ cup water + 1½ tsp dashi powder; for vegan/vegetarian, make Vegan Dashi)
  • 1½ Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp mirin
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ⅛-¼ tsp kosher salt (Diamond Crystal; use half for table salt)
  • ⅓ cup mentsuyu/tsuyu (concentrated noodle soup base) (I use a 1:7 ratio for mentsuyu to water. I use Kikkoman Koidashi Hontsuyu brand. You can also make my Homemade Mentsuyu recipe)
  • 2⅓ cup water
  • 1 Tbsp mirin
  • 1 green onion/scallion
  • Shichimi togarashi (Japanese seven spice) (for a spicy kick; optional)

Steps

To Cook The Udon Broth

  1. In a medium saucepan, add the dashi, mirin, sugar, soy sauce, and salt.
  2. Bring the broth to a gentle simmer. Once the broth is simmering, remove it from the heat. Cover with a lid and set aside.

To Cook the Quick Udon Broth (with Mentsuyu; optional)

  1. Follow your mentsuyu bottle instructions to make the broth.
  2. n a medium saucepan, combine the water, mentsuyu, and mirin. Bring it to a simmer over medium heat. Once simmering, remove the pot from the heat. Cover with a lid and set aside.

To Cook The Udon Noodles

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. When the water comes to a full rolling boil, add udon noodles and cook according to the package instructions. For fresh homemade udon, separate and untangle the strands of noodles with your hands, and cook for 10 minutes.
  2. Using chopsticks, stir the noodles so they won't stick on the bottom of the pot.
  3. Drain the noodles into a colander and run under cold water.
  4. Once the noodles are cool enough to touch, rinse the starch off. Then change the faucet to hot water and run hot water to warm up the noodles.

To Serve

  1. Serve the hot udon noodles in individual bowls. Pour hot broth over the noodles and top with chopped green onions and optional shichimi togarashi.
